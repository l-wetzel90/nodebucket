Nodebucket... the struggle

Nodebucket is a task management system. It is designed for work atmosphere with employees. This has been designed with expansion in mind. As it is right now the person logged in is the only one that can add, update and delete tasks. This could be extended out for management to use by adding employee roles. Management could then add and assign tasks.

My original idea was to have all the tasks in one area. Employees would log in and pick what they wanted to do. This would mark the task as in progress. When they moved it to done it would be updated for everyone. I overthought everything the first couple weeks and lost time to do this. This could be developed some more to add more functionality.

I learned a lot about myself while doing this project. I learned that I overthink too much. I just need to do whatever I am thinking and go back if it was wrong. I overanalyzed the file structure in the beginning. Every book we read for this program was by different authors. They all had different ways of programming. The instructional videos provided yet another way. And finally, the instructors had yet another way. Looking back on everything I tried to add a little of everything in, when all I should have done was do it my way. It would have all worked out in the end.

I enjoyed working on this application though. It showed what I really need to focus on as a developer and designer.
